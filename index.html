How do you calculate the estimated rental cost of a car?

We calculate the total rental cost by initially adding the rental amount per day multiplied by the number of days the customer intends to rent the car. Depending on the chosen car's price category, we apply a multiplier. For instance, if the car's price category is Premium, the price should be multiplied by two, and so on. If the driver has a driving license for less than 5 years, we increase the amount by 20%.

What happens if the driver has a license for less than 3 years and selects a Premium category car?

If the driver has a license for less than 3 years and chooses to rent a Premium category car, we inform them that they cannot rent that specific car.

How does the cost change when there are fewer than 3 available copies of a particular model in the rental agency?

If there are fewer than 3 copies of a specific model available in the rental agency, the cost should increase by 15%.

How is the fuel cost calculated, and how is it incorporated into the rental costs?

We calculate the fuel cost based on the estimated kilometers to be driven, the fuel price, and the average fuel consumption. This cost is then added to the overall rental expenses. Finally, the customer should see both the net and gross rental amounts, with the gross rental price being the net price increased by 23%. Additionally, we display a breakdown of the components, such as fuel cost, base rental price multiplied by the number of days, etc.
